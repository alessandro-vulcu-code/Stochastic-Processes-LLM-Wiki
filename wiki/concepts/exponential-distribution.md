# Exponential Distribution

**The exponential distribution is the continuous memoryless distribution and models Poisson-process inter-arrival times.**

## Definition

For $\lambda>0$,
$$
f_T(t)=\lambda e^{-\lambda t},\qquad t\geq 0,
$$
with CDF $F_T(t)=1-e^{-\lambda t}$, mean $1/\lambda$, and variance $1/\lambda^2$.

## Key Properties

- Memoryless survival:
$$
\mathbb{P}[T>s+t\mid T>s]=\mathbb{P}[T>t].
$$
- Inter-arrival times of a Poisson process are i.i.d. exponential.
- Minimum of independent exponential variables is exponential with rate equal to the sum of rates.

## Intuition

The distribution has no aging: surviving up to time $s$ does not change the law of remaining lifetime.

## Connection to Other Concepts

- [[poisson-process]] is generated by i.i.d. exponential inter-arrivals.
- [[gamma-distribution]] is the sum of independent exponential variables.
- [[renewal-process]] becomes Poisson when inter-arrivals are exponential.
