# Delayed Renewal Process

**A delayed renewal process has a first interrenewal time with a different distribution from all later interrenewal times.**

## Definition

Let $X_1$ have distribution $G$ and let $X_2,X_3,\ldots$ be i.i.d. with common distribution $F$, independent of $X_1$. The counting process generated by
$$
S_n=X_1+\cdots+X_n
$$
is a delayed renewal process.

## Key Properties

- Only the first cycle is special.
- Long-run renewal limits are typically unchanged by the initial delay.
- Markov-chain first-passage visits from $i$ to $j$ can form a delayed renewal process before ordinary $j\to j$ cycles begin.

## Intuition

The process starts out of equilibrium or from a special initial condition, but after the first renewal it behaves like an ordinary renewal process.
